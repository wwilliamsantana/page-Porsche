Porsche 911 GT3 RS

So what makes this 992 generation so much better you ask. It's a crazy stat - it actually produces twice as much downforce over the previous car. You read that right - a 100% increase in downforce. Truly a marvel from Porsche's engineers, and a signal from Porsche, this is a purist's car, track ready. Most of this is thanks to the GT3 R racecar, from which the RS was produced in conjunction with, hence a true track delight. For the first time, Porsche have fitted the GT3 RS with DRS (drag reduction system), which aids low drag on straights and high speed areas, much like the system seen in F1 cars. This flattens the wings, whilst an airbrake function is also present, to emergency brake.

The brakes are shared with the GT3, though the 16.1-by-1.4-inch iron front discs are 0.1 inch thicker. Carbon-ceramics are optional. Forged aluminum wheels are standard, wearing 275/35-20 rubber up front and steamroller 335/30-21s out back. The Weissach package unlocks the option of magnesium wheels. The Weissach package costs a whopping $33,250, and Porsche has released a configurator for the GT3 RS if you want to see all the options and personalize one for yourself. All the RS model's enhancements bring the starting price to $225,250 (before gas-guzzler tax), or about $63,000 more than a GT3.

Engine specifications

  • 4.0-liter flat-six
  • Naturally aspirated
  • 386kW / 525cv
  • 465 Nm / 47Kgfm

Performance

  • 0 to 100 km/h in just 3.2 seconds
  • Maximum speed of 296 km/h
  • 409 kg of downforce at 200 km/h.
  • At 285 km/h, the total aerodynamic load is 860 kg.

Body

  • Length: 4.572mm
  • Width: 1.900mm
  • Height: 1.322mm
  • Wheelbase: 2.457mm
